BMX Overview

BMX started in the early 1970s when children began racing their bicycles on dirt tracks in Southern California, drawing inspiration from the motocross superstars of the time.
Hover over the below categorys to see more information.

Race

BMX Racing is now in the Olympics as of 2010. The best things about Racing are;

  • Great for Speed
  • The Community
  • Being part of a Club
  • The ability to make friends for life
  • Events almost every weekend
  • Get to travel the world

Freestyle

Freestyle is one of the most impressive disciplines about BMX, it is now also in the Olympics. Heres a few things to highlight about this sport;

  • Multiple Diciplines inc. Ver, Park, Street and Dirt
  • Ride how you want where you want
  • Create or combine tricks to make your own style
  • There is no rulebook to follow
  • Utilise Foam Pits or Resi Boxes to master your tricks

Flatland

Typicaly descirbed as ballet on wheels. This form of BMX has been on of the most self expressive todate. See what makes this dicispline unique below;

  • Unique BMX frame designs
  • Consists of Spins, Bike Control and crazy variations
  • Manipulation of the body and physics to keep your bike stable
  • Lock in your balance point to get the most out of a certain trick
  • Creativity is key in this dicipline
